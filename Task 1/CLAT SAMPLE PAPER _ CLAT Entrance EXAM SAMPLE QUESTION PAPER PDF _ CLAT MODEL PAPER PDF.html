CLAT Syllabus(Undergraduate)


English - This section will have questions based on comprehension passages of 450 words each. In the comprehension section, candidates will be evaluated on their understanding of the passage and its central theme, wordings used, etc. candidates will have to read and comprehend the passage and summarize it in simple words. The comprehension passages are going to be followed by a series of questions supporting the passage. The candidate will be asked to comprehend meaning of various words and phrases to assess applicant's knowledge of grammar and vocabulary, word phrase and meaning etc.

Quantitative techniques- In this section, the questions will be asked from elementary Maths and the objective is to assess the candidate's basic ability to derive numerical information from passages, graphs, or other representations, As per the latest updating in the pattern, the Quantitative techniques section will firmly be based on the class 10th level including questions from ratios and proportions, basic algebra, mensuration and statistical estimation.

General knowledge & Current affairs- Questions in General knowledge and current affairs will also be in the form of a comprehension passage of 450 words each. Here candidates will be asked about the contemporary events of significance from India as well as from the world, arts and culture, international affairs and historical events of ongoing significance.

Logical Reasoning- In this section, candidates will be given passages of around 300 words each and each passage will have questions like analyzing patterns of reasoning, drawing conclusions based on evidence given, identify conflicts and similarities and assess the value of arguments.

Legal Aptitude- This section will contain the comprehension based questions of 450 words each. The passages will comprise topics from legal matters, public policy questions or moral philosophical enquiries. The section will contain prepositions and facts based problems.


CLAT Paper Pattern (Undergraduate)


English

  1. Read and comprehend the passage, set aside any arguments and viewpoints discussed in the passage;
  2. Draw conclusions based on the passage;
  3. Summarize the passage in simpler words;
  4. Compare and contrast the various arguments or viewpoints began within the passage; and
  5. Comprehend the meaning of various words and phrases used in the passage.
Quantitative Techniques
  1. Derive numerical information set out in the passages, graphs, or other representations;
  2. Make use of the basic 10th standard mathematical operations on the given information, including from areas such as ratios and proportions, basic algebra, mensuration and statistical estimation.
General Knowledge & Current Affairs

  1. Contemporary events of significance from India and therefore the world.
  2. Arts and culture
  3. International affairs; and
  4. Historical events of ongoing significance.
Logical Reasoning

  1. Identify an argument, its principles and conclusions
  2. Read and identify the opinions set out in the passage
  3. Critically analyze patterns of reasoning, and evaluate how conclusions depend on evidence you find.
  4. Assume what follows from the passage and apply these implications to new situations
  5. Draw relationships and analogies, identify conflicts and similarity, and assess the value of arguments.
Legal Reasoning

  1. Identify and gather the rules and principles set out in the passage;
  2. Apply those rules and principles to various fact situations; and
  3. Comprehend how changes to the rules or principles may vary their application to various fact situations.

Some important tips for CLAT exam preparations



Make an effective study plan:

Coaching Institutions will do their job at best to guide you, but you must make an effective study plan for your self-study if you want to crack CLAT.

Since Planning is the key to success, you must plan your study schedules to complete the syllabus with every concept clear in your head before examinations.

But of course, you have to design workable and practical schedules. Set some weekly schedules, plan for the subjects to complete within that week, and assign your time to them accordingly. Try to focus more on chapters that have greater weightage in marks and study them from time to time. Also, try to solve as many CLAT sample papers as possible.
Stay focused and you will be able to complete your course on time.

Extra effort on weaker areas:

Everyone has one or the other weak points, but smarter are the ones who work harder on their weak areas and make it their strongest point.

It is very important to attempt all the CLAT sample papers tests organized by online coaching institutions as it will help aspirants to try and find out areas in which they are weak.
Study them again which are difficult to understand at once. Work harder on your weak points. Practice more and more logical reasoning and passage-based questions. Try to revise vocab which is difficult to remember at once.

Don’t panic if you are making progress at a slower pace, it is your determination what matters so keep that up and study again.


Revise from time to time:

There is a vast syllabus to complete when it comes to preparing for CLAT but revision is also necessary. Coaching institutes always ensure that the students revise from time to time by taking mock tests and also provide students with previous year CLAT model papers.

Revision plays a big role in memorizing what you have learned so far and also it makes the concept clearer. So, revise periodically.

Never start any new topic a few days before the exam.


Solve previous year question paper:

Solving CLAT Sample question papers is the best way to map your preparations. It will give you the idea of the areas in which you have to work more and also help in improving your time management skills.

Institutes you are enrolled in will provide students with CLAT Sample question papers so that students get familiarised with the exam pattern and also the type of questions mostly asked in the examinations. Apart from these, many sites are offering to you their customized CLAT model Papers which are designed according to the latest pattern of the exam, aspirants must also try to solve them if their time allows.

Make your notes:

Making notes while you study is the best way to keep track of all the topics that you have covered and it will also help you in time of your revision.

Always try to write down new information that you read on any online platform in your notes.
Organize your notes and keep them updated while you cover the syllabus.


Take small breaks:

One cannot go on and on studying without taking breaks. It is not good for physical as well as mental health as it will continue to deteriorate if you don't take breaks. Therefore, take small breaks between your schedules, listen to songs, or take a small nap, but never extend it for long. Taking small breaks will keep your mind relaxed and will help you for better focus.


Attend all the sections:
Never focus on just one or two sections. It is equally important to pay attention to all the sections of the exam if you need to crack it.


Focus on vocabulary and grammar:
Learn new words and their meaning. Increase your vocabulary with extensive reading. Practice grammar on free time, to make sure the sentence formation and the structure of the sentences are correct.

Group discussions:
Group discussions help enhance one's knowledge. You can discuss with your other friends who are also preparing for the CLAT some topics or can discuss current affairs . Best online coaching classes for CLAT also organises online GD with experts and the faculty in which aspirants can take part and add on to their knowledge. This will help everyone participating in the GD learn something new which they may not know.


Positive attitude makes difference:

“A positive attitude always leads us to a positive outcome”. It is our attitude which makes the biggest difference to the kind of life we will lead.If you are patient enough to overcome your failures and your weak points throughout this journey of your CLAT preparations it will make a huge difference in the kind of result you will receive.
There are manifold advantages of solving CLAT model papers. It helps you stay focused, understanding the structure of the paper, helps improve time management skills and will also boost the confidence of the aspirants.
