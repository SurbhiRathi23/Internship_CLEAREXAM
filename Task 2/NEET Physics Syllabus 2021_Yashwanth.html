NEET Physics pattern:


There will be an almost equal weightage from the class 11 and class 12 physics subjects of the CBSE, and different state government boards. So, each candidate needs to focus carefully on the entire Physics syllabus for NEET during the preparation for the NEET exam. The total number of questions in the NEET Physics section is 45, which together carry total marks of 180 in the NEET Examination.

Understanding and applying the concepts of theory in the numerical problems is a key factor for scoring well in the NEET Physics section. The NEET Syllabus Physics has almost 19 topics from both the years, with almost similar weightage from both of these years’ topics. The Physics syllabus for NEET needs different approaches than that of the other subjects and needs special attention and focus or at least equal attention as the other two subjects get. The emphasis needed on the topics with numerical questions is very much required and necessary to rank among the top in the country.

The 3 hours pattern of the NEET paper, with 180 questions, and 45 each in physics and chemistry make it a very challenging competitive exam for NEET aspirants. The +4 mark for each correct answer and -1 for wrong answer makes it necessary for every candidate to attempt carefully to be ahead in the competition.
